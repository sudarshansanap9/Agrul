Bullock Cart

While modern transportation has largely replaced them in many regions, bullock carts remain symbolic of traditional farming practices and are occasionally still utilized for their simplicity and low environmental impact

Harvesting

Modern methods involve the use of machinery like combine harvesters for grains and specialized equipment for fruits and vegetables. Efficient harvesting practices are essential for maximizing yield and maintaining crop quality.

Seeder Rural Tool

Manually operated or attached to a tractor, depending on its size and design. This tool plays a crucial role in efficiently and uniformly sowing seeds across large agricultural fields, contributing to the overall productivity of crop cultivation.

Throwing

Broadcasting is a common method of throwing seeds by hand, distributing them evenly across the soil. In a modern context, throwing may also involve the use of equipment like broadcast seeders or fertilizer spreaders that mechanically disperse materials over a designated area. The goal is to achieve an even distribution, promoting optimal growth and yield for crops.

Plowing

This process helps loosen the soil, control weeds, and incorporate organic matter. TDifferent types of plows, such as moldboard plows, disc plows, and chisel plows, are employed based on the specific needs of the soil and the crops to be planted. Plowing is a critical step in soil preparation, promoting better aeration, water absorption, and nutrient availability for the subsequent planting and cultivation of crops.

Spraying

This can include the spraying of pesticides, herbicides, fungicides, or fertilizers. The goal is to evenly distribute the substances over the crops to protect them from pests or diseases, control weeds, or provide essential nutrients for growth. Proper spraying techniques are crucial for effective and targeted application, minimizing waste and maximizing the benefits to crops.
