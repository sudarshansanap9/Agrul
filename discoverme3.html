Drip Irrigation System

In this system, water is conveyed through a network of tubes or pipes to drip emitters, which release water in controlled and measured amounts directly at the base of each plant.

Sprinkler Irrigation

Sprinkler irrigation systems designed full coverage of an area or adjusted for specific crop requirements. They come in various types, including stationary, traveling, and center-pivot systems.

Centre Pivot Irrigation

These systems consist of a series of pipes, often supported by wheels, radiating outward from the pivot point. As the pivot moves in a circular pattern, water is distributed through sprinklers mounted on the pipes, irrigating the crops beneath it.

Furrow Irrigation Systems

Furrow irrigation is commonly used in row crops like cotton, maize, and vegetables. It is a relatively simple and cost-effective irrigation method, but it may lead to uneven water distribution, and water efficiency can be a concern.
